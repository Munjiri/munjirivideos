Creating Impactful Video Content for Purpose-Driven Brands and Charities

Ready to create videos that truly connect with your audience? It’s not just about showcasing your product—it’s about meeting your customers where they are on their journey and helping them find the right solution.

Understanding the customer journey is essential for any marketing strategy. It adds a deeper layer of insight into your audience’s needs and how they’re experiencing them as they move closer to buying. Sure, it’s great to know their goals and challenges, but the closer they get to a decision, the more their needs and questions evolve. And so, how you speak to them should evolve, too.

Now, if you search for “customer journey map,” you’ll find all sorts of complicated, multi-step processes. These are often based on big companies with complex sales funnels. But for most of us, we don’t need to get bogged down in every little step. We can keep it simple and focus on three core stages—discovery, consideration, and decision.

Here’s a breakdown of how you can plan your videos for each stage of the journey:


Beginning

This is where potential customers first encounter your brand. They might find you through social media, a Google search, or even Pinterest. The goal here is to spark curiosity and get them interested in exploring more.

What to Focus On:

Short, fun, and engaging videos that capture attention.
Content that aligns with your brand values, even if it’s not directly about your product or service.

Video Ideas:

Behind-the-scenes clips

Quick, educational or inspiring snippets (less than 30 seconds)
Playful collaborations or day-in-the-life videos

Best Platforms:

Instagram Reels, Pinterest, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

Middle

By this point, your audience knows about your brand, but they’re still deciding if you’re the right fit for them. This is your chance to nurture trust and build deeper connections.

What to Focus On:

Educational and insightful content that demonstrates expertise.

Offer solutions to common problems or share unique perspectives in your industry.

Video Ideas:

Q&A sessions to address common questions

Storytelling that highlights your brand's mission or values

Thoughtful product demos or “making of” videos

Best Platforms:

Your blog, YouTube, LinkedIn, email newsletter

The End

At this stage, your audience is almost ready to buy—they just need a little push. The right video can tip the balance and turn interest into a purchase.

What to Focus On:

Reinforce trust with social proof and clear value propositions.

Show how your product or service delivers results and solves problems.

Video Ideas:

Customer success stories or case studies

Testimonials and product demos

"What not to do" videos for a clever twist

Best Platforms:

Website product pages, sales pages

A Final Tip: Think in Circles, Not Straight Lines

The customer journey isn’t always a straight path—it loops back to discovery, advocacy, and repeat buying. Keep creating valuable content that inspires your audience to stay engaged and continue exploring your brand.
