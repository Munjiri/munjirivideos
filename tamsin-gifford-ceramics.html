A Morning with Tamsin Gifford Ceramics in the Ansião Countryside

Down a foresty path, past a mini tractor, fig trees some sheep and a small church is Tamsin's ceramic studio where we spent some time photographing her process and classes for her new website. Chatting, Terracotta pots spinning and Ruari was asleep under the orange tree.

"I am continually inspired by organic form and also history and archaeology. I like the simplicity of ancient functional pottery. inspiration for my forms , which are often organic and earthy."

She works mainly with stoneware clay, raw oxides and gold lustre. Enjoying abstract fast painting and decoration, spontaneous marks and touches of gold.
